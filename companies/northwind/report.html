Findings

What the evidence actually shows.

The detail behind the scores, grouped by where the work lives: reputation (off-site), content (citeable assets), and the site itself.

Reputation

2.4 / 5 · off-site graph

The root cause of the broken job. The reference layers that answer "what / who is Northwind" are adequate (Crunchbase, LinkedIn, a little funding press), but the discoverability-critical layers — the category roundups, community, and review sites models pull from — are thin or absent.

Absent from every "best stablecoin payment infrastructure" roundup. The single weakest, highest-leverage gap. Missing from 5 of 5 fetched category roundups — finextra, stablecoininsider, cobo, crossmint, startupstash — all led by Circle, Bridge, Sphere, Fireblocks, Brale. These are the exact pages Perplexity and ChatGPT pull from, which is why mention rate collapses to 33%.

Listicles — scored 1/5None of the checked roundups named Northwind. Perplexity (the most listicle-dependent surface) never names it at all.

Directories and funding press are a strength. A current, populated Crunchbase profile (founded 2021, NYC, ~$78M raised, named investors, ~90 staff) and an active LinkedIn page give a model clean, corroborating data. The 2025 Series B drew a funding-press cycle (TechCrunch, Finextra, PYMNTS).

Directories 4/5 · Press 3/5The reference layer that answers "what / who / how big is Northwind" is clean — this is why the assessment job is healthy.

No Reddit, no G2, no Wikipedia. Effectively zero organic Reddit presence in the threads models trust for vendor shortlists; no G2 or Capterra profile (both 404), so no citeable peer-review signal; no Wikipedia article, with "Northwind" resolving to the Microsoft sample database. These are cheap surfaces sitting empty.

Reddit 1/5 · Review sites 1/5 · Wikipedia 1/5A claimed G2 profile and a Reddit presence would create citeable signals where there are currently none.

Content

2.2 / 5 · citeable authority

Pages that exist, not yet a source models reach for. The product is explained clearly and there's a real developer-docs surface, but there's no original research, no named framework, and no research hub anchoring the category terms — the direct cause of the 11% citation rate.

The data exists — it's just never published. Northwind sees genuinely citeable proprietary data — payout volume by corridor, settlement times, conversion spreads — and publishes none of it as research. For a platform processing a multi-billion-dollar payout run-rate, the raw material for the definitive "state of stablecoin payouts" report is sitting unused, so models have no Northwind data to quote on the category.

Original research 2/5 · Named frameworks 1/5No recurring report, no index, and no named, ownable framework — the two assets that earn category citations.

No research hub, low cadence. Roughly one blog post a month, skewed to product updates and company news; explainers are scattered across the general blog with no indexable insights/research hub concentrating authority. The cadence doesn't read as a compounding body of work that would make the site the source models pull for "stablecoin payouts."

Publishing cadence 2/5 · Backlinks 2/5Earned links are almost entirely the funding cycle — there's no original content for third parties to cite as the source.

Site

4.3 / 5 · vetting hygiene

Cleanly vettable by a model — and the reason the assessment job is healthy. Content is fully server-rendered, accurate Organization / FinancialService / FAQPage / Person schema sits in the raw HTML, and a complete llms.txt maps the site.

No JS-gating, and the schema pins the facts. A no-JS homepage fetch returns the full headings, product names, the regulatory summary, and the founders. The schema is comprehensive — correct legalName, NYDFS BitLicense and MSB as credentials, FinancialService on product pages, plus FAQPage and Person markup — so the model never has to guess, and by-name answers stay accurate and current.

Fetchability 5/5 · Schema 5/5 · llms.txt 5/5FAQPage and Person schema are present — the gaps that let stale facts creep into other audits are already closed here.

Pricing is fully gated. Every conversion routes through "Talk to sales" / "Get API keys," no public tiers — expected for B2B infrastructure, but a model asked "how much does Northwind Pay cost" can only answer "contact sales." Directional proof points exist in marketing copy (per-transaction payout fee, FX spread on conversions) but aren't structured on the core pages.

Pricing clarity 2/514 "Talk to sales" CTAs on the homepage; no /pricing page; no parseable rates.
Prioritized fixes

Sequenced by what moves the broken job first.

Ordered so the highest-leverage discoverability work leads, with the cheap assessment touch-ups slotted last. Each fix is tagged by the job it serves and rated on impact and effort.

01

Get into the category roundups.

Via PR, outreach, and placement, land in the "best stablecoin payment infrastructure / payouts" listicles — finextra, stablecoininsider, cobo, crossmint, startupstash — currently led by Circle, Bridge, Sphere, Fireblocks, and Brale, with Northwind absent from all of them.

Answers: listicles 1/5, mention rate 0.33, Perplexity 0% mention — these are the exact pages the listicle-dependent surfaces pull from.

Discoverability Impact High Effort Medium
02

Ship the data product only Northwind can publish.

A quarterly "Stablecoin Payouts Index" — volume by corridor, settlement time, conversion spreads — as an ungated, HTML-first report on a real /insights hub with Dataset schema, so models have a Northwind source to cite on the category instead of competitor domains.

Answers: citation rate 0.11 and content authority 2.2/5 — converts a multi-billion-dollar data asset that's sitting unused into a steady citation flywheel.

Discoverability Impact High Effort High
03

Own the payment-flow narrative with a named framework.

Build one vendor-neutral, named framework ("The Stablecoin Payment Flow: pay-in, convert, payout") as a canonical pillar page plus definitive guides for each head term, then seed them into comparison content so the single-API full-flow positioning surfaces where models look.

Answers: named frameworks 1/5 and the PSP pay-in blackout (disc-2 = 1.0/5) — a reused, attributed framework is what earns category citations.

Discoverability Impact Medium Effort Medium
04

Claim the cheap third-party surfaces.

Stand up a G2 / Capterra profile with a handful of customer reviews and seed a category presence on Reddit, to create citeable signals where there are currently none and lift category share of voice.

Answers: reddit 1/5 and review sites 1/5 — low effort, surfaces models actively trust for vendor shortlists.

Discoverability Impact Medium Effort Low
05

Protect the healthy assessment job.

Two low-effort touches: add the missing FAQ entries (pricing, typical customers) and publish a directional pricing-model page, so by-name answers can cover cost instead of "contact sales." Don't over-invest — this job is already strong.

Answers: pricing clarity 2/5 and the one uncited Claude comparison — polish, not the lever that moves the broken job.
